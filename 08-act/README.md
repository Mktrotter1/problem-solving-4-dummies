# Step 8: Act

> *Do the thing.*

You've chosen. Now execute. This is the step everyone wants to jump to first — but when you've done steps 1-7, execution becomes dramatically simpler.

## What This Step Is

Acting means carrying out your chosen solution. The emphasis here is on doing it *deliberately* — not rushing, not overthinking, just executing the plan with attention.

## How to Do It

**Break it into steps if it's big.** If the action is complex, decompose it into smaller, concrete steps you can do one at a time. "Migrate the database" isn't an action — it's a project. "Back up the current database" is an action.

**Do the smallest meaningful thing first.** Start with something that gives you early signal about whether your approach works. Don't build the whole thing before testing whether the foundation is sound.

**Keep notes as you go.** What did you actually do? What happened? This feeds steps 9 and 10, and it's much harder to reconstruct after the fact.

**Stay on plan unless the plan is wrong.** If you encounter something unexpected, pause and evaluate. Is this a reason to change course, or just friction? Don't abandon a good plan because of a speed bump. But don't blindly follow a plan into a wall, either.

## Techniques That Help Here

### OODA Loop (Tight Execution Cycle)
During execution, run rapid observe-orient-decide-act cycles. Observe what's happening as you execute. Orient — does this match expectations? Decide — continue, adjust, or stop? Act on that decision. Repeat.

The faster you cycle through OODA during execution, the faster you self-correct.

### PDCA / Deming Cycle (for Iterative Actions)
If your solution is iterative:
1. **Plan** the next small step
2. **Do** it
3. **Check** the result
4. **Act** on what you learned (adjust the plan)
5. Repeat

### Timeboxing
Set a time limit for execution. "I'll try this approach for 2 hours. If it's not working by then, I'll reassess." This prevents sunk-cost spirals.

## Common Mistakes

- **Perfecting instead of doing.** Done is better than perfect. You defined "done" in step 5. Aim for that, not beyond it.
- **Changing plans mid-stream without reason.** Stick to the plan unless evidence shows it's wrong. Discomfort is not evidence.
- **Not tracking what you do.** You'll need this in step 9. Write it down.
- **Going dark.** If others are involved or affected, communicate what you're doing and what's happening. Silence breeds anxiety and duplicated effort.

## The Key Question

**"Am I executing the plan I chose, and is it working so far?"**

When you've completed the action, move to [Step 9: Check](../09-check/).
