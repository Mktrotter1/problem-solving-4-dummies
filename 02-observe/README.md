# Step 2: Observe

> *What's actually happening?*

You've noticed something is off. Now resist every urge to fix it. First, look.

## What This Step Is

Observation is gathering raw information without judgment, interpretation, or solution-seeking. You're building an accurate picture of reality before you do anything about it.

This is where most people fail. The instinct to jump to solutions is overwhelming. Fight it. You cannot solve what you have not seen clearly.

## How to Do It

**Describe, don't explain.** Say "the screen turns red when I click submit" — not "the validation is broken." Description is observation. Explanation is interpretation. You're not there yet.

**Gather facts.** What's happening? When does it happen? Where? How often? To whom? Under what conditions? What *doesn't* happen that you expected?

**Look for what's present AND what's absent.** The dog that didn't bark matters as much as the one that did.

**Write it down.** Your memory is unreliable. Observations you don't record are observations you'll distort or forget.

**Separate data from noise.** Not everything you see is relevant. But at this stage, err on the side of collecting too much. You can filter later.

## Techniques That Help Here

### The Scientific Method (Observation Phase)
Before hypothesizing, scientists observe and record. Systematically. Without agenda. Do the same.

### Active Listening
If the problem involves people, listen to understand — not to respond. Repeat back what you heard. Ask clarifying questions. "Tell me more about..." is your best tool.

## Common Mistakes

- **Observing through a theory.** If you already think you know the answer, you'll only see evidence that confirms it. This is confirmation bias, and it will wreck you.
- **Confusing observation with diagnosis.** "The database is slow" is a diagnosis. "Queries are taking 12 seconds instead of 200ms" is an observation.
- **Stopping too early.** The first thing you notice is rarely the whole picture. Keep looking.
- **Observing alone.** Other people see things you don't. Ask them what they see.

## The Key Question

**"What is actually happening, right now, that I can see/measure/verify?"**

When you can describe the situation factually and completely, move to [Step 3: Define](../03-define/).
